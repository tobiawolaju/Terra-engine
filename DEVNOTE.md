# DEVNOTE.md
This file has nothing to do with the code.

---

I saw this jam on a Thursday.

Deadline was Saturday.

I had no game. No assets. No team. Just a idea that came together in a conversation, a belief that it could work, and about 48 hours to prove it.

So I built it.

Not because I was ready. Not because the timing was perfect. Not because I had everything figured out. I built it because the window was open and I decided to jump through it.

---

If you're reading this inside this repo, maybe you're a developer. Maybe you're stuck on something. Maybe you've been working on your thing for months and it feels like nothing is moving. Maybe you've restarted so many times you've lost count. Maybe someone told you it wasn't realistic. Maybe you told yourself that.

I want you to know something.

Every hard thing you've been through was training.

Every project you abandoned — you learned something. Every late night that produced nothing — you built endurance. Every time you almost quit and didn't — you built the muscle that lets you finish things under pressure.

You were not falling behind. You were loading up.

---

This game is about a Terraformer stranded on a dying planet. They have to keep it alive until rescue comes. The rescue timer is real but it's long. Most people will never see it.

But someone will.

That's the whole point.

Some things are built for the long game. Some moments take longer to arrive than feels fair. That does not mean they are not coming. It means you have to keep the planet alive long enough to be there when the ship lands.

---

Don't give up on your thing.

Not today.

Your challenges are not punishment. They are not proof that you were wrong to start. They are the training ground for the moment that is coming for you specifically — the one that will require every hard thing you survived to get there.

Keep going.

Keep the planet alive.

Don't die.

---

*— Tobi*
